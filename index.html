Hello, I'm Rohit.

I am a PhD candidate supervised by Prof. Dr. Maren Bennewitz at the Humanoid Robots Lab, University of Bonn, and a member of the PhenoRob Cluster of Excellence. My journey in robotics is driven by a fascination with how machines can perceive and interact with the complex, uncertain world around them.

Currently, my research focuses on active perception, semantic mapping, and uncertainty-aware AI. I develop algorithms that allow robots—particularly in agricultural and horticultural settings—to autonomously decide where to look and how to move to build better maps and perform complex manipulation tasks, even when their sensors are faced with occlusion and noise on one of the sides.

My Journey

My academic roots began at SPCE, University of Mumbai, where I focused on electrical engineering. Seeking to bridge the gap between abstract control theory and physical machines, I moved to Germany to pursue an M.Sc. in Automation and Robotics at TU Dortmund.

A pivotal moment in my career was my Master’s thesis at the DLR - German Aerospace Center, where I worked on shared autonomy for assistive robotic hands. This experience solidified my interest in humanoid systems and human-robot interaction.

Before returning to academia for my PhD, I spent several years in the leading robotics hubs of Germany. At DFKI's Robotics Innovation Center, I contributed to EU-funded projects involving mobile manipulation and humanoid trajectory generation. Later, I joined NEURA Robotics as a Founding Engineer and Group Lead, where I spearheaded the AI in Manipulation team, bridging the gap between cutting-edge research and industrial-grade robotic products.

Today, at the University of Bonn, I bring these different worlds together—combining industrial rigor with academic curiosity to push the boundaries of what autonomous robots can understand.

Latest Update

Recently, I was honored with the Best Poster Award at the IROS Workshop on Perception and Planning for Mobile Manipulation (2025) for my work on Open-Vocabulary and Semantic-Aware Reasoning. I also had two major papers on evidential multi-task learning and global view planning accepted to the IROS 2025 main track.
